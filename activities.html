Rujul Gandhi

activites I engage in outside the classroom

Linguistics Olympiads

I enjoy designing and testing puzzles for language-based problem solving competitions. Currently, I'm chairing the international jury of the Asia Pacific Linguistics Olympiad and a member of the problem committee of the Panini Linguistics Olympiad.

MIT Educational Studies Program

MIT ESP is a student organization that hosts educational programs for school students with the principle: Teach Anything, Learn Anything. Being involved in ESP has opened me up to the world of large-scale program logistics, thoughtful policy discussions, and of course, education.

MIT Spinning Arts

Occasionally, I spin fire with MIT Spinning Arts. There is a lot to learn about movement and object manipulation in an environment of warmth and experimentation.

Classical dance

I've been learning and performing Bharatanatyam for the past eleven years under Guru Sandhya Dharm. I enjoy playing with rhythm as well as appreciating a variety of dance forms. I'm currently the president of Nritya, a new classical dance group at MIT.

The Tech

The Tech is MIT's undergraduate newspaper. I have been writing for the news department on a variety of issues that pertain to undergraduates or the Institute as a whole.
