Rujul Gandhi

Academic


Currently, I am an MEng student at MIT EECS.


I work in the Reliable Autonomous systems Lab at MIT (REALM), supervised by Chuchu Fan. People around me program robots to do useful things. I'm programming ways to tell robots to do useful things. Specifically, I am working on extracting actionable logical units from potentially ambiguous natural language requirements.


In winter and spring 2023, I will also work part-time on best practices in the development of AI systems, in a cohort supervised by Elizabeth Bondi-Kelley and Crystal Lee. This is under the SERC (Social and Ethical Responsibilities of Computing) Scholars program by the MIT Schwarzmann College of Computing.

Future

I would like my academic or career trajectory to explore a subset of the following directions:

  • understanding language
    what drives the evolution of form?
    how can we capture representation of meaning?
  • modeling language using computers
    what are the current capabilities of language modeling tools?
    how can we evaluate them in comparison with human language perception?
    what can computational models tell us about human language evolution?
  • designing better language technology
    how can we improve current language processing methods to work for low-resource settings and non-conventional communication?
    at the social level, how can we leverage language technology to reduce communication barriers?
